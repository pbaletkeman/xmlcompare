# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We expect all participants in this project and community to uphold the values of respect, inclusivity, and professionalism.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to creating a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Respecting the time and effort of maintainers and contributors
- Giving credit where it's due

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- Discrimination based on any characteristic protected by law

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces (code repositories, issue trackers, pull requests, etc.)
- All community spaces (chat, forums, events, etc.)
- Interactions outside of project spaces that are directly related to the project

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project maintainers. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Project maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the project's leadership.

## Consequences

Depending on the severity and pattern of behavior, consequences may include:

1. **Private warning**: A private message explaining the issue and expected behavior
2. **Public warning**: A public comment on the issue/PR explaining the issue
3. **Temporary ban**: Temporary removal from the project for a specified period
4. **Permanent ban**: Permanent removal from the project

## Appeal Process

If you believe you have been unfairly treated or believe a maintainer is not following this Code of Conduct, you may appeal by contacting the project leadership. Appeals should include relevant context and why you believe the decision was unjust.

## Contact

If you have questions or concerns about this Code of Conduct, please open an issue or contact the project maintainers through GitHub.
